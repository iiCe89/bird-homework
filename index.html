Why do birds have different beaks?

How do birds feet differ?

Most birds have four toes, with 3 facing forward and one back, but some birds toes are adapted to suit different needs. Many game birds, and domestic chickens, have a fifth toe with a sharp claw for fighting.

How are birds adapted to different habitats?

The feet of birds have evolved as an adaptation to the landscapes they inhabit. Wading birds, such as egrets and herons, have long toes to help with weight distribution as they make their way over reeds and lily pads. Ducks and pelicans have webbed feet which, much like SCUBA fins, make them more adept swimmers.
